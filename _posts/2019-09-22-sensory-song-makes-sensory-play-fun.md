Yes you heard it right. Tanya and Tanmay loves singing while their children are engaged in sensory play. They call these songs as, **Sensory song.** One of these songs, [“Round and Round”](https://www.enrichingearlylearning.com/sensory-play-to-engage-toddlers-sensory-roundabout/) is their favourite. These sensory songs not only enhance the experience of sensory play but also greatly helps in language and cognitive development.

###### Here is another song that you can sing for or along with your infants and toddlers:

On our body, we have 5 senses  
E-I-E-I-O  
On our face, we have 2 eyes  
E-I-E-I-O  
With a look, look here  
And a look, look there   
Here a look, there a look   
Everywhere a look, look  
On our body, we have 5 senses  
E-I-E-I-O  
  
  
On our body, we have 5 senses  
E-I-E-I-O  
On our face, we have 2 ears  
E-I-E-I-O  
With a listen, listen here  
And a listen, listen there   
Here we listen, there we listen,   
Everywhere we listen, listen  
On our body, we have 5 senses  
E-I-E-I-O  
  
  
On our body, we have 5 senses  
E-I-E-I-O  
On our face, we have a nose  
E-I-E-I-O  
With a sniff, sniff here  
And a sniff, sniff there   
Here a sniff, there a sniff  
Everywhere a sniff, sniff  
On our body, we have 5 senses  
E-I-E-I-O  
  
  
On our body, we have 5 senses  
E-I-E-I-O  
In our mouth, we have a tongue   
E-I-E-I-O  
With a lick, lick here  
And a lick, lick there   
Here a lick, there a lick  
Everywhere we lick, lick  
On our body, we have 5 senses  
E-I-E-I-O  
  
  
On our body, we have 5 senses  
E-I-E-I-O  
On our body, we have some skin  
E-I-E-I-O

And a touch, touch there   
Here a touch, there a touch  
Everywhere we touch, touch  
On our body, we have 5 senses  
E-I-E-I-O

This song always worked wonders for Sophie and Ryan. So you should try out with your children too.
