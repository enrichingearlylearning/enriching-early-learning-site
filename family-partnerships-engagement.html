FAMILY PARTNERSHIPS & ENGAGEMENT

Bridge Home and School, Together

Bring families into the conversation with an intentional, strengths-based partnership that support every child's social-emotional growth.

Perfect for standalone use or as an add-on to your Little Social Stars Bundle, this service helps you design family engagement that truly fits your center.

Schedule a discovery call Add to Little Social Stars

Why family partnerships matter?

Family engagement is more than newsletters and special events—it is a shared responsibility between families, educators, and the wider community to support children's learning and well‑being.

When families are meaningfully involved, children benefit from stronger social–emotional skills, improved behavior, and greater long‑term school success. For early learning programs, that means calmer classrooms, deeper trust, and a more cohesive support system around every child.

This service includes:

Together, we co‑create family engagement strategies that are realistic for your team and responsive to the families you serve.

Intentional Family Coffee Chats

EEL will be your new host for relaxed, welcoming conversations where families can connect with educators, ask questions, and share what works at home. These informal gatherings help build trust, open communication, and a genuine sense of community, especially for families who may feel unsure about traditional meetings.

Parent Workshops on Emotion Coaching

Support families with practical tools to respond to big feelings at home. Emotion coaching that helps children notice, name, and navigate emotions linked to better self‑regulation, resilience, and stronger parent–child relationships.

Workshops are interactive, accessible, and grounded in real life.

  • Understanding big feelings in the early years
  • Responding to meltdowns while staying connected
  • Building a shared language for feelings between home and classroom

Community Learning Circles

Bring families, educators, and community partners together in guided conversations around themes such as inclusion, belonging, behaviour support, and cultural celebrations.

Learning circles honour family voice, surface shared strengths, and move everyone toward a common vision for children's success.

Custom Home–School Partnership Strategy

Every center is different. We work alongside you to design a tailored family engagement plan that fits your staffing, schedule, and community context.

Your plan might include communication tools, survey questions, ongoing feedback loops, and simple, sustainable practices your educators can use to invite families into the learning process.

How It Works:

We keep the process simple and collaborative, so it feels doable for busy leaders and educators.

Step 1 – Discovery Conversation

We start with a conversation with your leadership team to understand your center's vision, current family engagement practices, and specific challenges.

Step 2 – Co‑Design

Together, we design the mix of supports that makes sense for you: coffee chats, parent workshops, learning circles, or a longer‑term strategy to strengthen home–school connections.

Step 3 – Implementation & Coaching

We provide facilitation, resources, and educator coaching so your team feels confident inviting families in, holding space for big conversations, and sustaining these practices beyond a single event.

Step 4 – Reflect & Refine

We review what's working, gather feedback from families and educators, and adjust your plan so that partnerships stay responsive and meaningful.

Perfect For

This service is perfect as a standalone offering or as an add‑on to your Little Social Stars bundle. It is ideal for early learning programs that want to deepen relationships with families, align home and classroom strategies, and center children's social–emotional well‑being.

You might be:

  • A childcare center or preschool seeking stronger home–school partnerships
  • A program implementing social–emotional or inclusion frameworks and wanting families actively involved in the journey
  • A leadership team noticing more behaviour challenges and wanting families and educators to feel "on the same team"

However you begin, we help you choose a starting point that feels realistic for your staff and accessible for your families—then grow from there.

What Families and Educators Gain

For Families

  • Practical tools to respond to big feelings at home
  • Increased confidence in supporting their child's social–emotional development
  • A clearer understanding of what is happening in the classroom and how to extend learning at home

For Educators & Centers

  • Stronger trust and communication with families
  • More consistency between home and school expectations
  • A more inclusive, collaborative culture that supports children's behaviour and learning
